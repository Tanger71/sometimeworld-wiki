Time Was [978]

The origin story of our protagonist ... and

Summary

Enter: Aition

Aition Grew up in a small fishing town on the east coast of The Mainland. Becoming tired of his life and having doubts about his future there, he joins a crew who is setting sale for new lands.

He eventually lands on a remote island with people. Their culture seems to be more primitive than back home: bronze age, tribal, hunter-gather. He settles for a time in a small {mountain village} and learns their language and ways. He shows the locals his steel tools, his sword, and tells them of his homeland. Wars, riches, nations, and Land: more land that could be possibly controlled by one King.

One particular local, Maqsud, takes exceptional attention to these stories.
"What if I could rule all that land?"
-> Branch into: The One King

The Neovenia seizes {mountain village} and only one person resists, perishing at the hands of Maqsud's own Edge, and leaving a young child as orphan. Aition knows this child as young child, and takes him under his wing as they make off for a new home, away from the Neovenia.

They wander island for several years. Over this time, the Neovenia begins to gain influence, making more Shuchu technology available. Most significantly, for Aition, Edges. Because the Edges burned white-hot, his steel sword became deprecated and no longer served him in protecting himself and {young child}. Since he was familiar with his steel sword, his transition to an Edge was more seamless than most, who were used to bronze spears and bows.

The Neovenia begins rapidly seizing power using their new fungal technology and religion to unite the island under one name {...}. Due to nationalistic and cultural instability on the island, Aition decides to return home and document his travels and discoveries back in the Mainland. He brings the boy because, just like his younger self, he doubts his future in Numentellus.

First Contact [1005]

This is partially inspired by the opening of Vagabond. A possibly entry point for a journey or ground zero explanation.

Summary

Oscar, an adolescent boy probably too young to fight, wakes up laying on the battlefield. Likely was knocked out and thought dead by both friend and foe very early on in the bout. The battle was over â€¦ seemingly, both sides were quiet. Whoever won the fight had likely moved on from the area leaving most of the dead behind, on the ground. Weird, most people would collect their dead and burn or bury them later. Oscar spots a fellow soldier close to his age, introduced as Tanner.

After greeting, the other boy points out Oscars injury. Oscar is confused because he doesn't seem to feel too much pain. It seems that his right arm is completely severed at the elbow and the open wound filled with this blue gunk, stopping the bleeding. He should definitely get that checked out ... but he does not feel any pain and seem to be in no danger of dying after all. The other boy suggests to scavenge the armour and weapons of the dead to sell or use in future battles. He notices that many of the dead laying in the mud have the same blue wounds as his.

After looking around, Oscar finds some weird looking gadgets he stuffs into his bag as well as a short sword he can wield with his non-dominant hand. As they traverse the battlefield, they test their new weapons by hacking on corpses and finishing off the mortally wounded. He follows Tanner for a bit, and comes across an healthy Invader.

Tanner jumps in front of Oscar, considering Oscar has only one arm, and is cut down with relative ease thanks to the invader's white-hot Edge.

Oscar steps back, in fear and awe at the invader and his wicked blade of light. He suddenly remembers the moments before he was knocked unconscious. He remembered being approached by this very invader and the beginning of the battle and being cut down before he could even process what was happening.

He drops his steel sword and raises his hands, understanding he is no match for his opponents sword of light. The invader retracts her blade of light back into her handle and stands by, analyzing Oscar's missing arm and recalling the preceding events. Seemingly impressed (by his affinity to the fungal interface), she approaches Oscar, picks up his sword and places it in Oscars hand. "She wants to fight me again".

Should Oscar lose his arm in the 1-1 bout with the invader, instead of the initial battle?

After a short exchange, Oscar is overpowered by the invader and held at edge-point on the ground. She goes for a final blow, which Oscar parries to the side with his steel sword. Before the invader is able to make her second attempt, she is interrupted by Aition who had noticed the exchange from the tree line and decided to intervene. They have a brief exchange where Aition ultimately slices her across the abdomen.

Oscar stands up and rips the retracted Edge from the invader's cold hands. Still rattled at the loss of his new friend and in awe of these light swords that he now possess, he follows Aition as they continue across the battlefield.

Oscar, puzzled at how these devices work, begins to ask about Aition and how he had learned how to use one and about the mysterious blue gunk.

Aition reacted standoffish to Oscar, still recently upset by the loss of {young child} and determined to reach the front line of the invasion. He tells Oscar he has no use for this device due to his missing arm and to return home and trade the device for ...

A loud bang is heard in the distance... likely gunpowder. Oscar is left behind in the middle of the forest as Aition continues forward towards it.
