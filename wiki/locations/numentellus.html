Numentellus (Aopuru)

(and its people)

Intro

Home of the Numentellian peoples. It is located to the East of the the mainland, Archaina.

Etymology

The name is derived from the Latin word numen (divine) and tellus (land, region). It was given to the island by Maqsud after the The Great Conversion and has a latin namesake due to his obsession with overseas culture.
Before being named Numentellus, it was referred to as Aopuru, Maori for ao (cloud, land) and puru (blue), named after the soft blue soil of the island.

Ecosystem

Numentellian are vegetarians. They benefit from the Shuchu fertilization property to get large crop yields. In return, they hunt for animals and leave their carcass to be absorbed by the fungus. In the local religion, it is treated at a sacrifice to their godly entity in return for crop fertility.

History & Politics

Historically, Numentellian have lived in smaller groups of people throughout the island relative peace aside from rare bouts over land and resources. Generally, thanks to the fungus, everyone was well fed and lived a life of excess. This was changed recently when a religious warlord, Maqsud, founded The Church of Neovenia and unified the island using Shuchu Technology. They did not stop there, colonizing lands overseas and eventually reaching the Archaina.

Technology

Apart from Shuchu technology, the Numentellian are otherwise quite primitive. Crutched by the support of the fungus, Numentellians never had the motivation or need to innovate along the traditional pathway as the rest of the world: traditional agriculture, steel, gunpowder. Instead, they have innovated within the path of the Shuchu, developing somewhat analogous and extensions to traditional technology. Most of their technology after the The Great Conversion is composed of wood, copper alloys, and fungal tissue. See Fungal Utility

It is thought that Numentellus diverged from the outer world and developed its own technological pathway just before the widespread use of steel tools and irrigation; They are still in the Bronze Age and primarily employ hunter-gatherer means of sourcing food due to Shuchu fertilization and the abundance of food.
